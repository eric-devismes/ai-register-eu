Regulatory Frameworks We Rate Against

Each AI system is assessed against applicable EU-wide and sector-specific regulatory requirements.

EU Regulation

EU Artificial Intelligence Act

Risk classification (unacceptable, high, limited, minimal), conformity assessment, transparency obligations, and prohibited practices under Regulation (EU) 2024/1689.

42 assessment criteria Effective Aug 2025
EU Regulation

GDPR — Data Protection

Assessment of AI systems against data processing principles, automated decision-making rights (Art. 22), DPIA requirements, and cross-border transfer safeguards.

28 assessment criteria In force since 2018
EU Regulation

DORA — Digital Resilience

ICT risk management, third-party dependency assessment, incident reporting capabilities, and operational resilience of AI-dependent critical functions under Regulation (EU) 2022/2554.

24 assessment criteria Effective Jan 2025

EBA / EIOPA AI Guidelines

Supervisory expectations for use of AI/ML in credit decisioning, insurance pricing, and anti-money laundering, including model governance and explainability requirements.

How Our Ratings Work

A transparent, structured methodology grounded in regulatory text and supervisory guidance.

1

System Identification

We catalogue the AI system's purpose, capabilities, deployment model, data inputs, and the sectors where it is deployed within the EU.

2

Risk Classification

Each system is classified under the EU AI Act risk tiers (unacceptable, high, limited, minimal) based on its intended use and Annex III categories.

3

Multi-Framework Assessment

We evaluate compliance against all applicable regulations — AI Act, GDPR, DORA, and relevant sector-specific frameworks — using standardised criteria.

4

Rating & Publication

Systems receive per-framework grades (A to D) and an overall compliance posture. Ratings are reviewed quarterly and after major regulatory updates.
